Animangas!!

What is animanga? The word "animanga" is a compound one. It is made up of 'anime' and 'manga'. The Japanese coined the term "anime." It alludes to animation, or cartoons as they are more often known. However, the word "manga" is an original Japanese word. Some of my favourites are (in the images above) are Jujutsu Kaisen by Gege Akutami, Chainsaw Man by Tatsuki Fujimoto, Nana and Paradise Kiss by Ai Yazawa, Blue Lock by Muneyuki Kaneshiro, Kamisama Kiss by Julietta Suzuki, Ouran High School Host Club by Bisco Hatori and Fruits Basket by Natsuki Takaya. I mostly enjoy the shoujo (manga intended for teen and tween girls) and shounen (manga targeting an audience of adolescent boys).

Animals!!

Another interest of mine are these animals in the image above!! The first animal is a seal. I think seals are very cute. Seals are a widely distributed and diverse clade of carnivorous, fin-footed, semiaquatic, mostly marine mammals. The animal on the top right is a raccoon. I enjoy watching videos of them on social media, I think they are a funny creature. The raccoon is a mammal native to North America. The animal on bottom right is a capybara!! My friends says the capybara reminds them of me for some reason LOL The capybara is a giant cavy rodent native to South America. Finally, the last photo is a picture of a shark!! Sharks are a group of elasmobranch fish characterized by a cartilaginous skeleton, five to seven gill slits on the sides of the head, and pectoral fins that are not fused to the head. I think sharks are a very misunderstood animal, as they are commonly known as killer fishes. But there are only around 80 unprovoked attacks yearly are reported worldwide, which is quite rare.
