A family gives people a sense of identity, support, and belonging, making it a key social unit. People initially pick up beliefs, social skills, and cultural customs from their families. Families can take many different shapes; they can be nuclear, adopted, or extended, and each has its own dynamics and relationships. The love, concern, and respect that family members have with one another transcends structural differences. Families provide some of the most durable and strong links in the world, influencing people's lives and future relationships. Family is a steadfast source of support and strength during both happy and difficult times, encouraging personal development and emotional health.
